**2024 British Grand Prix: How Pit Stop Timing Decided Hamilton's Home Victory**



The 2024 British Grand Prix at Silverstone was defined by changing conditions and, above all, by the precise timing of pit stops, which ultimately enabled Hamilton to claim victory at his home race. The two key transitions – from dry to intermediate tyres, and then back to dry tyres – decided much of the race.



At the start, George Russell maintained the lead, with Lewis Hamilton and Lando Norris close behind. In the early laps on dry tyres, Russell and Norris ran consistent lap times between 1:30.5 and 1:31.5. Hamilton posted very similar times, though slightly slower at some moments due to tyre management. Ferrari’s Charles Leclerc was stuck in the high 1:32s, showing greater degradation.



Further back, the midfield battle heated up quickly. Nico Hülkenberg ran consistent lap times around 1:32.0–1:32.5, allowing him to pressure the group ahead. Behind him, Fernando Alonso and Lance Stroll fought for position, with gaps of less than a second for several laps. Sergio Pérez, meanwhile, remained stuck behind that train, running low 1:33s and losing time to the cars ahead – a deficit that would prove difficult to recover later.



The first key moment came around lap 25, when rain began to fall. The difference between drivers became immediately clear. Norris still managed a high 1:31 on one of the first damp laps, but lap times quickly dropped: Russell and Hamilton fell to 1:35–1:37, while some midfield drivers, such as Hülkenberg, posted times as high as 1:38–1:40 on slick tyres.

Oscar Piastri was among the first to pit for intermediate tyres. He immediately set times in the low 1:36s, significantly faster than those still on slicks. This difference – up to 4–6 seconds in a single lap – allowed him to gain several positions. In contrast, Norris and Mercedes waited one more lap, losing that crucial time.



In the midfield, this decision had an even greater impact. Yuki Tsunoda and Alexander Albon pitted at the right moment and gained many positions, while other drivers who stayed out too long, such as Stroll, lost over 8–10 seconds in a single lap and dropped out of the points battle.



Once the track was fully wet, lap times stabilised. Norris led, running mid 1:35s, with Piastri close behind in low 1:35s. Hamilton remained in the 1:36s, not losing too much ground. During this phase, Max Verstappen began his recovery drive, running consistently in the 1:35–1:36 range while others made mistakes.



The second key moment came around lap 40, as the track began to dry. Intermediate tyres were no longer optimal, and drivers like Norris were lapping in the 1:35–1:36 range. Hamilton, who pitted at the right moment for slick tyres, immediately set a high 1:32, gaining over 2–3 seconds compared to those yet to pit.



Norris pitted one lap later, and during that extra lap he lost roughly 3–5 seconds – enough for Hamilton to overtake him. Piastri also lost time after his stop, mainly due to traffic with slower cars.



In the midfield, this second transition again created major gaps. Tsunoda and Albon timed their pit stops correctly once more and consolidated their positions, while other drivers who hesitated fell out of the points contention.



In the final laps, Hamilton controlled the race with lap times around 1:31–1:32, while Norris ran slightly slower and could no longer recover the lost time. Verstappen completed his comeback with a highly consistent performance, making no mistakes during either transition. Silverstone 2024 showed, once again, that in changing conditions, hitting the stop is worth more than the pure rhythm.
